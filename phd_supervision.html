    2017–2021: Manon Sabot, University of New South Wales
    Manon's PhD will explore drought extremes in the 21st century. She plans to focus on how we represent the impact of drought on the vegetation in state-of-the-art climate models.

    2015–2018: Ned Haughton, University of New South Wales
    Ned's PhD explores the predictability of land surface fluxes. In particular, his research focuses on the performance of land surface models.

    2015–2018: Jinyan (Jim) Yang, Western Sydney University
    Jim's PhD focusses on modelling the carbon uptake of Australian evergreen ecosystems under rising [CO2] and water limitations. Jim recently has his first paper accepted: "Applying the concept of ecohydrological equilibrium to predict steady-state leaf area index". His PhD will also focus on how we model the exchange of carbon and water fluxes at the EucFACE experiment, as well as improving the representation of stomatal conductance at high vapour pressure deficit in models.
